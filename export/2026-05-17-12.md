# Biomedical Knowledge Graphs
## RAG-GNN: Integrating Retrieved Knowledge with Graph Neural Networks for Precision Medicine
- **Url**: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00586v2
- **Authors**: ['Hasi Hays', 'William J. Richardson']
- **Abstrat**: Network topology excels at structural predictions but fails to capture functional semantics encoded in biomedical literature. We present RAG-GNN, an end-to-end trainable retrieval-augmented graph neural network framework that integrates GNN representations with dynamically retrieved literature-derived knowledge through a jointly optimized retrieval projection, gated fusion mechanism, and contrastive alignment. In a cancer signaling case study (379 proteins, 3,498 interactions, 14 functional categories), RAG-GNN improves functional clustering from silhouette $= -0.237 \pm 0.065$ (GNN-only) to $-0.144 \pm 0.066$, a consistent improvement of $+0.093 \pm 0.022$ across 10 random seeds, while the learned retrieval achieves mean precision@10 $= 0.242$, a 152\% improvement over the random baseline ($0.096$). Heuristic information decomposition with bootstrap confidence intervals reveals that topology and retrieval encode overwhelmingly shared information (95.6\%), with retrieval improving both intra-cluster cohesion (silhouette) and cluster agreement (ARI $+0.021 \pm 0.015$). Counterfactual experiments confirm that adversarial, absent, and random retrieval all degrade performance, validating that the gated fusion mechanism depends on document content. Benchmarking against eight established embedding methods demonstrates task-specific complementarity: topology-focused methods achieve strong link prediction, while retrieval augmentation consistently improves functional clustering within the controlled GNN-only ablation. DDR1 subnetwork analysis provides confirmatory validation consistent with established synthetic lethality relationships. These results establish that topology-only and retrieval-augmented approaches serve complementary purposes for precision medicine applications.


**Summary**:

- (1):精准医学需整合基因组、蛋白质互作网络等异质数据，但现有网络拓扑方法（如GNN）仅编码结构信息，无法捕获生物医学文献中的功能语义（即“结构-功能鸿沟”）；

- (2):过去方法包括DeepWalk、Node2Vec、LINE、Spectral以及GCN、GAT、GraphSAGE等GNN模型，它们擅长链路预测等结构任务，但无法编码文献中的功能语义；且固定模式的知识图谱（Knowledge Graphs）难以处理非结构化文本。本文提出RAG-GNN，首次端到端联合优化图神经网络（GNN）表示与检索增强生成（RAG）文献知识的融合，通过可训练的检索映射、门控融合机制（Gated Fusion）和对比对齐（Contrastive Alignment）解决上述问题，动机明确；

- (3):(a) 提出联合优化目标及理论保证；(b) 对8种嵌入方法进行系统基准测试，揭示任务特异性表现；(c) 通过互信息分解（95.6%共享信息）与反事实实验验证检索的真实增益；(d) 在癌症信号网络案例中证明检索增强持续改善功能聚类；

- (4):提出端到端可训练的RAG-GNN框架：包括联合优化的检索投影（Retrieval Projection）以从文献中检索相关文档、门控融合机制（Gated Fusion）以融合GNN表示与检索知识、以及对比对齐（Contrastive Alignment）以协调表示空间；

- (5):在癌症信号网络（379个蛋白质，3498条边，14个功能类别）上，功能聚类的轮廓系数（Silhouette）从GNN-only的-0.237±0.065提升至-0.144±0.066（提升+0.093±0.022）；检索精度Precision@10达0.242，较随机基线提升152%；链路预测与节点分类任务上拓扑方法更优。性能有效支持其目标。


## Graph-Grounded Optimization: Rao-Family Metaheuristics, Classical OR, and SLM-Driven Formulation over Knowledge Graphs
- **Url**: http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12204v2
- **Authors**: ['Madhulatha Mandarapu', 'Sandeep Kunkunuru']
- **Abstrat**: We propose graph-grounded optimization: a paradigm in which the decision variables, constraints, and objective coefficients of a real-world optimization problem are sourced from a property knowledge graph (KG) via Cypher queries, rather than supplied as free-form natural-language text or static tabular input. We motivate the paradigm by surveying recent LLM/SLM-driven optimization systems -- OptiMUS, Chain-of-Experts, LLMOPT, OPRO, FunSearch, Eureka -- none of which consume property graphs as the primary input modality. We instantiate the paradigm in the open-source samyama-graph database and evaluate seven real-world public-domain KG-backed problems spanning drug repurposing (245K-node biomedical KG), clinical-trial site selection (7.78M-node trial registry), Indian supply-chain rerouting (5.34M-node OSM road graph), healthcare equity allocation (WHO/GAVI/IHME KG), economic-environmental grid dispatch, antimicrobial-resistance stewardship (NCBI AMRFinderPlus, 10.4K resistance genes), and wildfire evacuation routing (OSM Paradise, CA). We compare a portfolio of Rao-family metaheuristics (BMWR, Jaya, SAMP-Jaya, EHR-Jaya, Rao-1) against Google OR-tools (CP-SAT and GLOP) reference solvers. We find that (i) no single Rao variant dominates: BMWR wins on discrete-with-tradeoff and high-dim-with-hard-constraint problems while Rao-1 wins on continuous low-/mid-dim problems, empirically supporting a portfolio approach; (ii) OR-tools dominates on small linear/MILP-friendly sub-problems but cannot encode the non-linear objectives that emerge in several of the real-world settings; (iii) graph-grounded formulations surface data-quality issues (missing properties, degenerate aggregates) that purely text-formulated optimizations would silently mask


**Summary**: 

- (1):本文的研究背景是现实世界的优化问题（如医疗、供应链、能源）通常来源于异构的Property Knowledge Graph (KG)，而非整洁的数学公式或自然语言文本。现有的优化系统大多依赖人工书写数学规划或基于大语言模型（LLM）的文本转混合整数线性规划（Text-to-MILP）管道。

- (2):过去方法包括基于LLM/SLM的系统（如OptiMUS、Chain-of-Experts、LLMOPT、OPRO、FunSearch、Eureka），它们将自由形式自然语言描述转换为优化模型。存在问题：(i) 自然语言是对底层数据的损耗性编码，数值约束被近似处理；(ii) 当底层数据变化时，LLM需重新消歧。本文提出的Graph-Grounded Optimization与现有方法不同，它直接从Property Knowledge Graph (KG) 通过Cypher查询获取变量、约束和目标系数，而非使用自然语言文本。该方法通过源头锚定数据图，避免了文本编码的损耗，并能自动感知数据质量问题和变化，动机充分。

- (3):本文贡献包括：(i) 提出图锚定优化（Graph-Grounded Optimization）新范式；(ii) 在开源数据库samyama-graph上实例化该范式；(iii) 在七个真实世界大规模KG问题（如245K节点生物医学KG、7.78M节点试验注册图、5.34M节点OSM道路图）上进行系统评估；(iv) 比较了Rao族元启发式（BMWR、Jaya等）与Google OR-tools（CP-SAT和GLOP）的表现；(v) 揭示了数据质量问题会被图锚定公式化暴露而文本公式化掩盖。

- (4):本文研究方法论是：将决策变量、约束和目标系数通过Cypher查询直接从Property Knowledge Graph (KG) 中抽取，形成图锚定优化问题。随后采用Rao族元启发式（包括BMWR、Jaya、SAMP-Jaya、EHR-Jaya、Rao-1）作为求解器，并与经典OR工具（Google OR-tools的CP-SAT和GLOP）进行对比。

- (5):本文在七个任务上评估：药物重定位（245K节点KG）、临床试验地点选择（7.78M节点）、印度供应链改道（5.34M节点OSM路网）、医疗公平分配（WHO/GAVI/IHME KG）、经济-环境电网调度、抗微生物耐药性管理（10.4K耐药基因）、野火疏散路线（OSM Paradise, CA）。性能表现：(i) 无单一Rao变体占优——BMWR在离散含权衡和高维硬约束问题上胜出，Rao-1在连续低中维问题上胜出；(ii) OR-tools在小规模线性/MILP友好问题上占优，但无法编码非线性目标；(iii) 图锚定公式化暴露了数据质量问题。性能支持了采用组合策略（Portfolio Approach）的目标。
